Arizona Department of Veterans' Services

Outdated agency website gets a much needed UX/UI makeover

Project Overview

The AZDV is a state agency that provides critical, state-wide coordination and technical assistance to services and organizations serving Veterans. The current website is antiquated and confusing. Although it is responsive, it has the look and feel of a much older site, navigation is not intuitive, and information is hard to find.

THE SOLUTION:
A complete website reorganization and rebuild. Special attention was paid to creating visual continuity and ease of navigation.

MY ROLE:
UX/UI Designer, Individual Class Project

TOOLS USED:
Figma, Adobe XD, InVision, and Affinity Designer

TIMELINE:
4 weeks

RESEARCH
Usability Testing

During the research phase I conducted 5 user interviews to gain a better understanding of how easy and intuitive the site was navigation was. Users were also given the specific task of finding the “Tuition Waiver Form” page and eligiblity requirements.

Proto Persona

Based on current US veteran demographics provided by the Veterans Administration (https://www.va.gov/vetdata/veteran_population.asp), I created a Proto Persona to help focus on a typical user and keep his/her needs and limitations in the forefront of the design process.

With almost 70 pages, and countless external links the website seemed to be growing in many different directions at once.
Information Architecture

An updated site map reduced the main navigation links from 7 to 5 and moved the staff login to a more prominent position in the universal header in the top right corner.

A universal footer was created to house common links such as “Contact Us", “Search”, social media links and legal disclaimers.
